I used Claude and ChatGPT. I use ChatGPT for general questions (Google-search style) and Claude Pro for more advanced problems, because I trust its output better.

I used ChatGPT before starting the assignment to give me a crash course on quality assurance for bluetooth software products. That took around 1 hour.

Assignment 1 best reflects my independent reasoning. This part was done without AI to begin with. When I was finished, I asked Claude for feedback and made some changes which are marked with an asterisk. This took about 30 minutes.

I used AI the most while doing Assignments 2 and 3 with the Wireshark logs. I had limited experience in this area, so I asked Claude for some strategy tips on how to analyse the logs and what the different protocols do. My initial strategy was to copy-paste chunks of the log into Claude, but I did not feel that was a good strategy, since it started to over-analyse and change its theories for every new bit of code I added. It recommended filtering the logs for different protocol types and trying to analyse each channel independently and then piece them together. I adopted this strategy and filtered the logs, created an overview of communication partners for each device over each protocol, the type of messages being sent over each protocol and then fed those notes to Claude to help me interpret. This strategy worked much better and gave me more meaningful results. This process took around 2 hours.

I used Google search to validate the AI-generated outputs when using it for general questions. For more specific tasks (like interpreting Wireshark log timelines) the output was not validated by other methods than my experience.
